## Chapter 7: Advice for Small Businesses Starting with AI
**Timestamp:** [19:25] - [24:07]

- Start with tasks your team hates - they won't pour heart and soul into dreaded work
- Track every task for 2-3 weeks before automating anything
- Use the autonomy spectrum: order use cases from least risky to most risky
- AI agents will fail - the goal is building muscle memory with low-risk wins first

**Lessons**
- Ask three questions: Does it require judgment/relationships/creativity? Does it follow predictable patterns? What's the cost of getting it wrong?
- Don't skip foundational steps and jump straight to building agents
- Document standardized processes before trying to automate them
- 2026 will be messy with valid info and misinformation - don't just copy what big companies claim to be doing

**Stories**
- Companies that are smart in 2026 are treating AI as foundational steps, learning AI features in existing tools before building custom automations

**Examples**
- Back and forth emails as automation candidates
- Social media posting (tedious but necessary)
- Data entry
- Start with existing tool AI features, then solo automation experiments, then agents
